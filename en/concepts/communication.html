Concept · Illumination of Existenz

Communication

existenzielle Kommunikation — der liebende Kampf

1 · In one sentence

The essential

No one becomes themselves alone: the truth of a life passes through at least one relationship in which two beings dare to be entirely truthful with one another — what Jaspers calls "the loving struggle".

2 · The experience

A moment everyone knows

We talk all day long, and we communicate very little. There is useful talk ("can you pick up the kids?"), social talk (the weather, colleagues, holidays), and debate, where each side pleads its case like a lawyer. All of this is normal and necessary. But everyone also knows the peculiar loneliness one can feel in the middle of all these words: surrounded, chatting, and met by no one.

And then there is — rare, unforgettable — a conversation of another nature. One in which we stop managing our image. In which we say what we had never yet put into words, and discover it in saying it. In which the other neither rushes to console us nor tries to win, but answers us with a frankness that would wound if it were not loving. We leave such conversations a little more real than we entered them. That is the raw material of this concept.

3 · The thought

What Jaspers makes of it

Jaspers advances a strong thesis: Existenz (what I can authentically be, that ground in me which decides and answers) does not awaken in isolation. I become myself only with another — never without them, never against them, never through a role. Hence perhaps his most famous formula: truth is what binds us together. A "truth" that I alone could see, and had submitted to no one, should always worry me.

Existential communication has precise conditions, and here Jaspers becomes strikingly concrete. It requires equality of level: no master and disciple, no saviour and saved — two beings at eye level. It requires reciprocity of risk: each exposes themselves; no one stays sheltered behind their persona. And it takes the paradoxical form he names the loving struggle (der liebende Kampf): a struggle, because in it we question each other without complacency; loving, because we struggle not against the other but for them — so that their truth and mine may come to light. In this struggle, he says, all weapons are laid down: no superiority, no tactics, no victory. If either wins, both have lost.

Why is this so central? Because without communication, every other concept goes bad. Freedom (that choice in which I choose myself) becomes solitary caprice. Faith becomes fanaticism — the certainty that no longer needs anyone is exactly what Jaspers fears most. Even boundary situations (death, suffering, guilt) bear fruit only when shared: a bereavement crossed in truthful speech transforms; a walled-up bereavement isolates. Communication is the vital medium of his whole philosophy — and Jaspers knew what he was talking about: his entire thought, he said, was born of his dialogue with Gertrud.

4 · Resonance

Living with this idea

First question, very simple: where, in my life, is that relationship? With whom am I entirely truthful — no image management, no role? If the answer is "no one", that is not a condemnation: it is a compass. A single relationship of that quality changes a life; it requires less time than honesty.

Second practice: in the next serious disagreement — with a partner, in the family, at work — try the rule of the loving struggle just once: do not try to win. Lay down the weapons: give up scoring points, arguing in bad faith, punishing with silence. Replace the question "how do I make them admit it?" with "what have I not yet understood?". It is difficult, almost against nature. It is also, precisely, what Jaspers calls philosophizing — for truth, real truth, exists only between two. Everyone may recognise here what their own tradition, religious or not, says about dialogue: this concept belongs to no camp.

Not to be confused

What it is not

  • "Communications" (media, marketing, networks): transmitting messages is not revealing oneself to another.
  • A debate to be won: if either wins, both have lost — existential communication knows no victory.
  • Fusion or perfect agreement: it is a loving struggle, not a dissolution of differences.
  • A mere exchange of information: two people can tell each other everything and never meet.

What it is: truthful speech between two equals who risk their personas to become themselves through one another.
