Karl Jaspers

Oldenburg 1883 — Basel 1969

“To philosophize is to be on the way.”

A physician turned philosopher, a witness to the shipwrecks of his century, Jaspers never taught a system: he lit up a path. This site hopes to make that path walkable — for everyone.

The man

A life that embodies his thought

Before the concepts, there is a man. A heart patient given a short life expectancy in his youth, who turned fragility into a method. A psychiatrist who understood that a human being can never be reduced to a diagnosis. A professor dismissed by the Nazi regime, who refused to leave Gertrud, his Jewish wife — they kept poison within reach, so as never to be separated.

His biography is not an appendix to his philosophy: it is its living proof. Every concept in the constellation above, Jaspers first lived through.
