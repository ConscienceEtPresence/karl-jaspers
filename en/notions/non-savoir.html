Notion · To go further

Non-knowledge

das Nichtwissen

1 · In one sentence

The definition

The lucid recognition of what cannot be known. Not a provisional ignorance that progress would fill: the point beyond which any assertion would become illegitimate.

2 · Put simply

If it were explained to you at fifteen

There are two ways of not knowing. The first is ordinary: I do not know the population of Peru — I can look it up, and in ten seconds I shall know. That is ignorance, and it fills in.

The second is of another kind. No one knows, and no one will know, whether the universe has a meaning. It is not that we lack data or instruments: it is that the question is not of a kind that data can settle. Non-knowledge means knowing exactly where you crossed from the first sort to the second.

A streetlamp lights a circle on the pavement. Looking for your keys inside the circle is reasonable. Looking for them outside it is hard. But looking for the night itself with the streetlamp is not hard: it is meaningless — the night is not a thing lying somewhere.

3 · An everyday example

The notion in action

A child asks: “why am I me and not someone else?” The embarrassed adult generally answers one of three things: a joke, a biological explanation (“because your parents…”), or “we don't know yet”.

All three dodge. The joke flees; biology answers a different question — it explains how this body appeared, not why it is me looking out of it — and “not yet” suggests some future laboratory will settle the matter, which is false. The honest answer would be: “that is a real question, and it has no answer that can be proven. No one will ever have one. You can think about it all your life.”

It is shorter, and infinitely more respectful — of the child and of the question alike.

4 · In depth

What Jaspers really makes of it

This concept follows directly from the structure of the Encompassing. We know only objects — that is our way of grasping, the subject-object split. Yet what is at stake in the decisive questions is precisely not an object: neither the Encompassing, nor Existenz, nor Transcendence. Non-knowledge is therefore no accident of our scientific history: it is a consequence of what we are.

What counts is that Jaspers draws from it the opposite of what one expects. Non-knowledge does not weaken thought: it frees it. Since these questions do not belong to knowledge, ceasing to treat them as problems awaiting solutions finally allows us to approach them as what they are — questions one lives by, not riddles to be solved. One does not solve death. One answers it.

There is a demand here, and it cuts both ways. Against the scientist who promises that science will eventually explain everything: no, some borders do not retreat, because they separate not the known from the unknown but two orders. Against the believer who claims to know what lies on the other side: no again. Non-knowledge forbids exactly both — and that is what allows it to speak to either.

5 · Not to be confused · Reading Jaspers

The right word, and where to read it

  • Ignorance: it is filled by research. Non-knowledge is not — there is nothing to find, because there is no object.
  • Scepticism: the sceptic doubts everything, including the provable. Non-knowledge vigorously defends knowledge, within its domain.
  • Obscurantism: it demands not less rigour but more — rigour is what draws the border.
  • Resignation: it is not giving up thought. It is ceasing to ask a question in a language where it has no answer.

What it is: a knowledge — the knowledge of knowledge's borders. And it demands more honesty than any assertion.

Where to read it in Jaspers: Way to Wisdom (1950); The Perennial Scope of Philosophy (1948); Von der Wahrheit (1947).
