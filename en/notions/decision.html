Notion · To go further

Decision

der Entschluss

1 · In one sentence

The definition

The act in which a possibility becomes an orientation of life. A real decision does not merely choose what I shall do: it decides who I shall be from now on.

2 · Put simply

If it were explained to you at fifteen

We make hundreds of decisions a day, and almost none of them is one. Choosing a dish, a train, a paint colour: these are preferences. They can be calculated, they are forgotten, and anyone in our place could have settled them the same way.

And then there are the others — three or four in a lifetime. They have an unmistakable sign: no one can make them in your place, no datum will carry them, and afterwards you are not quite the same. These are not bigger preferences. They are something else.

A junction on a railway line. The train does not choose to go fast or slow: it chooses a direction, and that direction, two hundred kilometres on, will have changed everything. The gesture lasts a second. What it commits lasts years.

3 · An everyday example

The notion in action

A woman has hesitated for two years over leaving a comfortable post. She made the lists, consulted people, compared salaries, read accounts. Nothing settles it — and not for want of information: she has more than enough.

See why. No datum can weigh security against the feeling of losing oneself. These are not two quantities: they are two lives. The two-column table will never help, not because it is badly made, but because the question is not of a kind calculation touches.

On the day she resigns, no one can say what changed — not even her. No new argument appeared. She decided. And there lies the whole difference between a preference, which follows reasons, and a decision, which cuts through them.

4 · In depth

What Jaspers really makes of it

Jaspers places decision exactly where knowledge stops. Consciousness in general — the one that proves and calculates — can illuminate a situation: its risks, its probabilities, its consequences. It will never decide, and not out of weakness: this is not a matter of knowing. Even knowing by heart the mechanism of one's own decision, one would still have to decide.

Hence the link with freedom, which is the heart of it: in a real decision I do not merely choose among options — I choose myself. What I decide and who I become are the same act. That is why such moments are so rare and so heavy: they do not consume a possibility, they make someone.

And here is the paradox that must be dared, because it is constant in him: at the moment the decision is most mine, I do not feel I invented it. “I could not do otherwise” — not through constraint, but because something imposed itself unconditionally. The freest decision has the shape of a necessity. It is through that fissure that his philosophy opens onto what exceeds us.

5 · Not to be confused · Reading Jaspers

The right word, and where to read it

  • A preference: it follows reasons; the decision cuts through them. Choosing from a menu is not choosing oneself.
  • An impulse: impulse forgets by tomorrow. Decision commits who you will be.
  • A conclusion: no reasoning produces a decision — otherwise we should all be transformed by our reading.
  • The result of a calculation: if a table suffices to settle it, it was not a decision. It was an operation.

What it is: the one act no one can perform in my place — the one in which what I choose and who I become are one.

Where to read it in Jaspers: Philosophy II — Illumination of Existenz (1932), on will and freedom; Way to Wisdom (1950).
