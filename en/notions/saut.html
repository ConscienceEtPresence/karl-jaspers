Notion · To go further

The leap

der Sprung

1 · In one sentence

The definition

The passage no continuous reasoning produces: in the boundary situation, one can tip from the existence that merely lasts toward the Existenz that answers. Nothing triggers it automatically.

2 · Put simply

If it were explained to you at fifteen

Some changes happen by degrees: you learn a language word by word, you heal a little each day. And some changes do not happen like that. Between “I know I shall die” and “I really know it”, there is no gradual progression. There is a tipping, or nothing.

The leap is that kind of passage. You do not approach it in small steps. You can think about it for twenty years without anything moving — and then, one day, something gives way, and you are no longer quite the person who was thinking.

No one learns to swim by reading. You can know the movements, the physics of buoyancy, everything — and stay at the edge. At some moment you have to let go of the rail. That instant is not the continuation of the reasoning: it is something else, and reasoning has never produced it in anyone.

3 · An everyday example

The notion in action

A man has known for ten years that his job is emptying him. He talks about it, analyses it, even has the exact words for it. Nothing moves. This is not cowardice: the reasons to stay are real — the salary, the family, his age.

Then one morning, with no new argument having appeared, he resigns. Ask him why today: he will not be able to answer. Nothing changed in the reasons. Something changed in the one who was weighing them.

The leap is there, and notice what it is not: it is no impulse — it had been ten years in the carrying. And it is no conclusion — no reasoning led to it. It is the third term, the one we have no word for — except this one.

4 · In depth

What Jaspers really makes of it

The leap has a precise place in the architecture. Boundary situations (death, suffering, struggle, guilt) are walls: they do not resolve. Before them, only two ways out, with nothing in between — veiling one's face, or tipping over. The leap is the name of the second. It carries one from Dasein, the existence that wants to last, to Existenz, that in me which answers.

What must be seen is why it cannot be continuous. Reasoning produces conclusions; it does not produce decisions. Between the best analysis in the world and the act there is always a step analysis does not take — otherwise we should all be transformed by our reading, and we know perfectly well that we are not. This is also why Jaspers does not write demonstrations but appeals: he cannot produce the leap in his reader, he can only make it possible.

And it must be said to the end, without making it heroic: nothing guarantees it. A whole life can unfold without a single leap — informed, busy, intelligent, and never decided. The boundary situation wakes no one automatically; it offers an occasion, and most often we decline. That is what makes the word so sober in him: it is not a promise, it is a possibility.

5 · Not to be confused · Reading Jaspers

The right word, and where to read it

  • An irrational flight: it is not giving up thought. It is a change in the way of existing, with open eyes — often after years of thinking.
  • An impulse: impulse forgets by tomorrow; the leap commits who you will be from now on.
  • A mystical conversion: there is no illumination, no new certainty, no revelation. Often nothing shows from outside.
  • A conclusion: no reasoning leads to it. That is exactly what defines it — otherwise there would be no leap, but a deduction.

What it is: the discontinuous passage toward Existenz — made possible by the boundary situation, made obligatory by nothing, and which no one can take in my place.

Where to read it in Jaspers: Philosophy II — Illumination of Existenz (1932), on boundary situations; Way to Wisdom (1950).
