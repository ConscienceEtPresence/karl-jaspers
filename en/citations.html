Quotations

Short, sourced, and tied to what they mean

An isolated quotation says almost nothing — which is why each one here leads to the page that explains it. Where the German is established, it stands alongside: Jaspers writes a tightly packed language, and English always loses something of it. Where a formulation is our own rendering, it says so plainly (“after”): we do not reproduce published translations, which belong to their translators.
