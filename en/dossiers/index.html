Long journeys

The dossiers

Taking the time of a whole book, chapter by chapter

The “concept” pages enter an idea through one door. The dossiers follow a whole book of Jaspers, from beginning to end, in about ten chapters. Each follows exactly the same route: a question, an answer in thirty seconds, Jaspers's thought, a metaphor, a concrete situation, the hardest objection and its reply, what it changes for you — then a sourced quotation, a short quiz and the related notions.

Read in order or dip in. And one rule is held throughout: what science says today and what Jaspers says as a philosopher are always distinguished.

Available

Two dossiers

  1. At the origins of man and historyAfter The Origin and Goal of History (1949). Ten chapters: from obscure prehistory to the Axial Age, up to the question of the meaning of history. When does a living being become a being who knows that it exists?
  2. The map of the EncompassingJaspers's conceptual heart. Eight chapters: the subject-object split, the four modes of what we are, world and Transcendence, reason and communication. Why can I never step outside the whole to look at it?
