Dossier · after Vom Ursprung und Ziel der Geschichte (1949)

At the origins of man and history

When does a living being become a human being who knows that they exist?

This dossier follows one of Jaspers's vastest books, The Origin and Goal of History. It is not a mere chronology: at every step one question works through the narrative — when, and how, does a living creature become a being who knows that it exists, that it comes from a past, and that it will die?

Ten chapters, from obscure prehistory to the question of the meaning of history, by way of the enigma of hominisation and the great turning point of the Axial Age. Each chapter follows the same route: a question, an answer in thirty seconds, Jaspers's thought, a metaphor, a concrete situation, an honest objection, what it changes for you, then a quotation, a short quiz and the related notions. Read in order, or dip in.

One rule of method, held throughout: what science says today (prehistory, evolution) and what Jaspers says as a philosopher are always distinguished — he was writing in 1949, and his strength lies not in the data, but in the question he puts to it.
