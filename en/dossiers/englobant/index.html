The map of the Encompassing

Why can I never step outside the whole in order to look at it?

This is the conceptual heart of Jaspers — and the dotted ring that surrounds the constellation on this site. The Encompassing is the horizon that contains everything and is contained by nothing: never an object in front of me, always that within which I already stand. One cannot walk around it; one can learn to find one's bearings in it.

Eight chapters, from the initial problem to the complete map. We shall see why Jaspers needed to forge this concept, what the subject-object split is that everything starts from, then the modes of the Encompassing one by one: the being we are (empirical existence, consciousness in general, spirit, Existenz) and the being that is (world, Transcendence) — and finally what puts all these modes in relation: reason and communication.

A warning from the outset: this is the most abstract dossier on the site. Each chapter therefore starts from a very concrete experience and ends with a question that concerns you. If a word resists, never mind — the map is understood all at once, at the end, when the pieces come together.

The eight chapters

  1. Why this concept?The problem the Encompassing answers: we cannot step outside the whole to see it.
  2. The subject-object splitI who look, what I look at — and what makes that separation possible.
  3. Empirical existence (Dasein)The living creature I am: the body, need, life that wants to last.
  4. Consciousness in generalThe mind that thinks and proves — interchangeable, universal, and unable to tell me who I am.
  5. SpiritThe world of ideas and meaning: culture, the work, the whole in which I breathe.
  6. ExistenzThe mode that never becomes an object: what I can authentically be.
  7. World and TranscendenceThe Encompassing that being is: what surrounds me, and what carries all without being carried.
  8. Reason and communicationWhat links all the modes — and the complete map, at last legible.
