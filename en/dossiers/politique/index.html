Dossier · nine chapters

Politics

After The Atom Bomb and the Future of Man (1958) — subtitled “political consciousness in our time”

People think this book is about the bomb. Its subtitle says otherwise: political consciousness in our time. The bomb is not its subject — it is the situation that finally forces the question, because it makes lying impossible. Five hundred and twenty pages, written in 1958, when Jaspers was seventy-five: this is his book of politics.

He opens by warning his reader of something nobody puts at the front of a book: there is no solution. He offers none, he has no plan, and he will have nothing to sell in the final chapter. What he is after is more modest and more difficult — to illuminate a situation, until whoever sees it can no longer act as though they did not.

Honesty is owed about what you are going to read. The book has three parts. The second describes the world of 1958 — Russia, America, Europe, the end of colonisation. It is dated, and we do not follow it: this dossier rests on the first part (violence, ethos, sacrifice) and on the third, which is pure philosophy and has not aged a day. There you find reason against understanding, total knowledge as flight, modern gnosis, democracy as education.

A further warning: this dossier contains the most disturbing position in his entire work. It is in chapter 9, and we will not hide it.

  5. What do the researchers think?April 1957: eighteen German physicists sign, and refuse.
  6. Understanding and reasonThe distinction that governs the whole book. Being clever is not enough.
  7. Total knowledgeWhoever knows where history is going has already excused himself from acting.
  8. Reason and democracyThe sovereign people is answerable for what it does. Democracy is an education.
  9. The turn cannot be plannedNo solution, no programme. And yet he refuses despair.
