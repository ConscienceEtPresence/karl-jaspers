Jaspers in everyday life

“I am in conflict with someone”

It has been going on for months. You are right, he is wrong — or the reverse depending on the day. And you already know that even winning, you will have won nothing.

What Jaspers will not say

That a good person avoids conflict. That harmony is the normal state and argument a failure. Jaspers refuses that picture of the world, and refuses it harshly: whoever believes you can live without struggling has not yet lived, or is having others carry his struggles.

What he offers

A distinction that saves years. There are two struggles, and from a distance they look alike. The struggle for something: winning, being right, taking the place. It has a victor, and the victor is left alone with his victory. And what he calls loving struggle: two people confronting each other without sparing each other, where neither seeks to win — each wants the other to become what they can be, and for that you must say what no one else dares. It is the opposite of kindness. You do not spare someone you take seriously. The test is simple and it hurts: do you want to win, or do you want him to exist?
