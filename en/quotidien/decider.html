Jaspers in everyday life

“I cannot decide”

You made the lists. You asked people. You read, compared, weighed. And you are exactly where you were six months ago.

What Jaspers will not say

That you lack information. That you should listen to your heart. That you need a table with two columns.

What he offers

Jaspers would say something more disturbing: if no datum settles it, perhaps no datum can. There are choices that cannot be calculated — not because they are too complicated, but because they are not of a kind calculation touches. The sign is sharp: no one can make it in your place, and you know it. Seeking still “more information” is then no longer prudence: it is the most respectable way of not deciding. What you lack is not a fact. It is an act — and it will come from nowhere but you.
