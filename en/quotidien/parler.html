Jaspers in everyday life

“I can no longer talk to him”

You still speak. The salt, the schedule, what someone said. And there is a room, in the house, that nobody has entered for a long time.

What Jaspers will not say

That this is a communication problem to be fixed with techniques. That you need to express yourself better, to use “I” statements. Jaspers does not believe in the toolkit for a second: you can apply every method in the world and meet no one.

What he offers

He names the real reason, and it is unpleasant: the fear of communication. We fall silent not for lack of time or words. We fall silent because showing yourself truly means letting someone see what you are — and they might not stay. So we prefer a dead but safe bond to a living but risky one. It is a calculation, and it is almost always the right calculation in the short term. Then he adds what we do not want to hear: you only become yourself in communication. Not first yourself, then connected. Both together, or neither. Which means the closed room protects nothing: it takes from both of you something you could only have had together.
