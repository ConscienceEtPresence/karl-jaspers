Jaspers in everyday life

“I analyse myself endlessly, and nothing changes”

You know everything. Your childhood, your mechanisms, why you do that, where it comes from. You could give a lecture on yourself. And nothing moves.

What Jaspers will not say

That you have not dug deep enough. That you are one session, one book, one insight short. Jaspers, who was a psychiatrist, will never say the solution is a little more analysis.

What he offers

He has a word for what you are living, and naming it already helps: dissolving self-reflection. Understanding yourself is necessary — without it you are the plaything of what you do not know, and he does not say otherwise. But it has a property we do not see: it has no floor. Every explanation can itself be explained, every motive has a motive behind it. Pushed far enough, it dissolves what it examines — nobody is left, only causes fitting into each other. And then analysis becomes exactly what it seemed to fight: the best way of deciding nothing, with the clear conscience of someone working on themselves. What stops it is not one more explanation. It is an act — something you do, which no analysis will produce, because it is precisely what analysis defers.
