Glossary

Jaspers's whole vocabulary — and the door to go further

This page is made to be revisited. A word comes back without its content? Look it up: its definition in three lines, the original German term, what it must above all not be confused with — and the link to the page that explains it in full. The glossary is complete: the central notions as well as the rare terms.

A

Absolute

das Absolute

That which depends on nothing else and which nothing limits. For Jaspers it can never be possessed as an object of knowledge: any claim to define it entirely manufactures a false knowledge.

Not to be confused with mathematical infinity. The absolute is not a magnitude: it is that before which every magnitude ceases to be a measure.

Transcendence →

Absolute consciousness

absolutes Bewusstsein

The inner certainty by which Existenz experiences an unconditional demand — in conscience, love, fidelity, decision. Certain for me, and never imposable on anyone.

Not to be confused with a proof. It commits the one who feels it; it compels no one else. That is exactly what separates it from fanaticism.

Philosophical faith →

Apparatus

der Apparat

The technical, administrative and industrial whole that lets millions of lives be organised. It is necessary — but it has one law: to function, it must make everything calculable, ourselves included.

Not to be confused with a modern evil: it is born in Sumer with the first scribe who inscribes a debt. What modernity adds is scale.

The spiritual situation of the age →

Appeal

der Appell

The form Jaspers's speech takes: it does not demonstrate Existenz, it addresses it. A philosophical text does not merely transmit information — it awakens, or it fails.

Not to be confused with a slogan or a moral exhortation. The appeal commands nothing: it makes possible an answer that remains entirely free.

Existenz →

Appropriation

die Aneignung

The act by which one does not merely learn a thought but tests it in one's own life. To read a philosopher is not to repeat their answers: it is to enter their questions.

Not to be confused with agreement: one can appropriate a thought by fighting it. What counts is that it commits you, not that it pleases you.

The path →

Atheism

der Atheismus

Refusal or absence of belief in God. Jaspers distinguishes critical atheism, which refuses objectified images of God — and which he respects — from dogmatic atheism, which claims to know that no Transcendence is possible.

Not to be confused with a single position. The first atheism is an ally of intellectual integrity; the second is dogmatism turned inside out.

Philosophical faith →

Atom bomb

die Atombombe

The new fact that changes everything: for the first time humanity holds the means of its own abolition. No technical solution will solve a problem that is not technical.

Not to be confused with one weapon among others. It makes everyone's survival each person's responsibility — a boundary situation on the scale of the species.

The spiritual situation of the age →

Authenticity

die Eigentlichkeit

Fidelity to what one recognises as true at the deepest level, without playing a role or hiding in common opinion. It proves itself in decision and communication, not in feeling.

Not to be confused with spontaneity. Doing whatever comes to me is not being true: authenticity demands clarity, not looseness.

Existenz →

Authority

die Autorität

A power recognised as legitimate because it serves a truth, a living tradition or a shared responsibility. It becomes domination the moment it demands obedience without permitting criticism.

Not to be confused with force. Authority that has to make itself obeyed has already ceased to be one.

Guilt →

Axial Age

die Achsenzeit

Between 800 and 200 BC, without knowing of one another, China, India, Iran, Israel and Greece see the rise of people who step back from received myth. The axis of history — because we still live by it.

Not to be confused with a golden age to restore, nor a West-centred narrative (it is the opposite). A fruitful hypothesis, debated by historians — never a hierarchy between peoples.

The Axial Age →

B

Being

das Sein

That which is. The question does not concern particular objects but the fact that anything is at all. No single theory can grasp it entirely.

Not to be confused with the set of all things. Being is not the sum of what exists: it is what cannot be walked around.

Being-in-itself

das Ansichsein

Being as it is independently of my knowledge. It never becomes directly accessible: every object already appears within the subject-object split.

Not to be confused with a thing hidden behind appearances. It is no backworld: it is the limit of objectification.

Dossier · The subject-object split →

Being-oneself

das Selbstsein

The always unfinished accomplishment by which one becomes oneself in freedom and communication. One becomes oneself only with others — never by withdrawing.

Not to be confused with self-assertion. Being oneself is neither doing as one pleases nor listening to oneself: it is answering.

Existenz →

Border zone

das Grenzgebiet

The space where a science reaches its limits and meets philosophical questions. It permits no slackening: it obliges us to distinguish sharply knowledge, hypothesis and belief.

Not to be confused with a no man's land. On the contrary, it is where intellectual integrity is most demanded.

Where to place Jaspers →

Boundary situations

Grenzsituationen

The walls that can be neither fled nor changed: death, suffering, struggle, guilt. Not accidents along the way, but the very structure of existence. Before them: close your eyes, or finally become yourself.

Not to be confused with mere hard blows. A problem can be solved or bypassed; the boundary situation has no exit by worldly means.

Boundary situations →

Bureaucracy

die Bürokratie

Organisation by procedures, offices and divided tasks. It becomes alienating when no one feels responsible for a decision produced by the apparatus.

Not to be confused with an excess of rules. The real danger is not heaviness: it is that responsibility dissolves, with no culprit anywhere.

The spiritual situation of the age →

C

Choice

die Wahl

Selection among several possibilities. It becomes existential when it no longer concerns what I shall do, but the person I become in doing it.

Not to be confused with decision (see that entry). Not every choice commits: choosing from a menu is not choosing oneself.

Freedom →

Ciphers (of transcendence)

Chiffren der Transzendenz

Realities of the world — a starry night, a work of art, an old story, a failure — that begin to signal toward what exceeds the world. A script whose complete key we shall never have: it suggests, it never proves.

Not to be confused with numbers or codes (the word renders the German Chiffre: a sign to be read). Neither a code to crack nor a decorative symbol.

Ciphers →

Compelling knowledge

das zwingende Wissen

What any mind must admit if it follows the method correctly: a demonstration, a reproducible measurement, an established fact. Compelling by evidence — never by power or by numbers.

Not to be confused with majority opinion, authority or sincere conviction. And it will never say what to live for, whom to love, or how to die.

Conscience

das Gewissen

The voice by which I examine my acts and recognise my responsibility. It reduces neither to social rules nor to psychological discomfort.

Not to be confused with fear of punishment, nor guilt in the psychological sense. It judges even when no one is watching — especially then.

Guilt →

Consciousness in general

Bewusstsein überhaupt

The mode of me that thinks, calculates and demonstrates. Because it brackets what I am, it holds for everyone — and I am replaceable: any instructed mind would obtain my results. It is the condition of the universal.

Not to be confused with psychological consciousness. It knows objects — hence everything about me as object, and nothing about me as me.

Dossier · Consciousness in general →

Consciousness of Being

das Seinsbewusstsein

The awareness that what is always exceeds the objects we know. Philosophizing aims to awaken it, not to pile up knowledge.

Not to be confused with one more piece of knowledge. It is not knowing Being: it is ceasing to believe that objects exhaust what is.

Criminal guilt

kriminelle Schuld

Responsibility for a specific violation of law, established by a court on facts and evidence. Strictly individual.

Not to be confused with a collective matter. No people, family or group is criminal as a whole — crime is always someone's.

Guilt →

D

Dasein · Empirical existence

Dasein

The first mode of what I am: the living creature, with this body, here and now. Its law is to last; its criterion, the useful; its horizon, the world as resource or danger. The ground of everything else.

Not to be confused with Heidegger's Dasein (where the word means the human being in all their depth). Here it is the most elementary mode.

Dossier · Empirical existence →

Decision

der Entschluss

The act in which a possibility becomes an orientation of life. A real decision does not merely choose what I shall do: it decides who I shall be from now on.

Not to be confused with a preference. No one can make it in my place, and no datum will carry it — that is how you recognise it.

Freedom →

Demonic

das Dämonische

The obscure, excessive, destructive forces that run through people, collective passions and history. Jaspers refuses to believe that all human reality can be made transparent to reason.

Not to be confused with the devil or the supernatural. It is an observation of lucidity: something, in us and in history, resists all rational explanation.

The spiritual situation of the age →

Determinism

der Determinismus

The thesis that our acts are entirely produced by our genes, childhood, milieu, era. Jaspers acknowledges every one of these determinations — he refuses them the last word.

Not to be confused with a proven fact: that everything is determined is a metaphysical belief, not a laboratory result. Two wagers, no proof.

Dossier · Nature, culture and freedom →

Dignity

die Würde

The value of the human being as possible freedom. It depends neither on health, nor intelligence, nor social usefulness, nor success.

Not to be confused with merit. It is precisely what cannot be earned and cannot be lost — otherwise it would be a wage.

Existenz →

Dogmatism

der Dogmatismus

The moment a limited truth takes itself for the whole truth and ceases to be questionable. It closes communication: whoever believes they possess has nothing left to learn from anyone.

Not to be confused with firm conviction. Dogmatism is not believing strongly — it is mistaking your faith for knowledge.

Philosophical faith →

E

Encompassing (the)

das Umgreifende

The central concept: the horizon that contains everything and is contained by nothing. Never an object before me, always that within which I already stand. Six modes: empirical existence, consciousness in general, spirit, Existenz — then world and Transcendence.

Not to be confused with the universe, nor the sum of all that exists: that would still be an object, a very large one. It is the background that never becomes a figure.

Dossier · The map of the Encompassing →

Existential communication

existenzielle Kommunikation

Truthful speech between two equals who risk their personas to become themselves through one another. Without it, no existential truth holds: no one becomes themselves alone.

Not to be confused with “communications”, debate, or a mere exchange of information — two people can tell each other everything and never meet.

Communication →

Existential truth

existentielle Wahrheit

Truth lived in a decision, a fidelity, a communication. It is not demonstrated like a formula — and it is no caprice either: it is verified in the coherence of a life.

Not to be confused with an opinion. You recognise it because it commits and transforms the one who carries it. An opinion costs nothing.

Truth →

Existenz

Existenz

What I can authentically be: that in me which decides, answers and becomes true. The only irreplaceable mode — no one can be it in my place. Jaspers speaks of possible existence: I can spend my life missing it.

Not to be confused with personality, character, the ego, or the soul in the religious sense. It never becomes an object: it is not known, it is decided.

Existenz →

Explanation

das Erklären

Seeking the objective causes of a phenomenon: a lesion, a molecule, a mechanism. Indispensable — and incapable of saying what the explained thing means to the one living it.

Not to be confused with understanding (see that entry). The best scanner shows your brain while you listen to music — never what that music does to you.

Dossier · Man and nature →

F

Facticity

die Faktizität

Everything given to me without my choosing it: my birth, my body, my era, my family, my past, my death. Freedom does not abolish it — freedom appears in how I take it on.

Not to be confused with an excuse. “That's just how I am” describes facticity and dodges the question: what am I making of it?

Freedom →

Faith

der Glaube

A certainty that commits existence without resting on compelling proof. Genuine faith knows doubt; the faith that refuses it becomes dogmatic, or brittle.

Not to be confused with a weak opinion. It is not a knowledge awaiting proof: it is another relation to truth.

Philosophical faith →

Finitude

die Endlichkeit

The condition of a being limited in time, knowledge and power. Not a defect: it is what makes decision, commitment and responsibility possible.

Not to be confused with a curse. An infinite being would have nothing to decide — the limit is what gives our acts their weight.

Boundary situations →

Foundering

Scheitern

The limit of every human construction: works, loves, knowledge, life itself eventually fail. But failure crossed with open eyes becomes the deepest cipher — where human power ends, something can speak.

Not to be confused with the cult of failure, nor nihilism. It speaks only to those who genuinely built, loved, risked.

Foundering →

Freedom

Freiheit

Not a state one possesses, but what one becomes in the rare choice where one chooses oneself. It is not proven: it is attested in the act. And at its summit, a paradox: I experience receiving myself.

Not to be confused with the absence of constraints, nor arbitrariness (a weathervane is not free), nor choosing among prefabricated options.

Freedom →

G

General Psychopathology

Allgemeine Psychopathologie

His 1913 book, written at thirty, still used by psychiatrists. In it he examines the methods that allow psychic phenomena to be described, understood, explained and classified.

Not to be confused with one psychiatric treatise among others. Its novelty is methodological: no single theory can explain the whole of psychic life.

The man →

Genius

das Genie

The capacity to produce a singular spiritual work. Jaspers studies it without irrational worship: what interests him is how a work opens a possibility for everyone.

Not to be confused with pathology. He wrote on great creators who were ill without ever reducing the work to the symptom.

The man →

Guilt (the four kinds)

die Schuldfrage

Four distinct planes, each with its own judge: criminal (the law, the court), political (the state whose citizen I am, reparation), moral (my acts, my conscience), metaphysical (human solidarity, before Transcendence).

Not to be confused with diffuse shame. No people is criminal as a whole: crime is always individual.

Guilt →

H

Historical consciousness

das Geschichtsbewusstsein

The capacity to recognise oneself as heir of a past, actor of a present, responsible for a future. Time passes for every living thing; man alone knows where he comes from.

Not to be confused with knowing dates. It is the lived experience of being situated — and therefore committed by what one received.

Dossier · The birth of history →

Historicity

die Geschichtlichkeit

The fact that I become myself only from an origin, a language, an era, ties I did not choose. Existenz is never an abstract freedom hovering outside time.

Not to be confused with history as a discipline. It is my condition of being dated and situated — and it is in that, not despite it, that I can become someone.

Dossier · The birth of history →

History

die Geschichte

The human process of memory, transmission, rupture, decision. It is not the unfolding of a law: no mechanism guarantees its future.

Not to be confused with chronology. Events happen to everything alive; history begins when someone remembers and interprets.

Dossier · Does history have a meaning? →

Human greatness

die menschliche Größe

The capacity of a human being to reach an exemplary depth of thought, creation or existence. In The Great Philosophers he is less interested in doctrines than in the forms of greatness they embody.

Not to be confused with prestige or success. Greatness is measured by what it opens for others, not by what it obtains.

The man →

Humanity

die Menschheit

The whole of human beings understood as a possible community of communication, responsibility and destiny. It exists biologically; its unity remains to be made.

Not to be confused with a given. Humanity is not an accomplished fact: it is a task, and it can fail.

Dossier · The unity of humanity →

I

Idea

die Idee

A totality of meaning that guides thought without ever becoming a finished object. It links particular knowledge and directs it toward a whole that does not close.

Not to be confused with an opinion or a concept. Jaspers opposes thought guided by an idea to the accumulation of exact and empty information.

Dossier · Spirit →

Illumination of Existenz

Existenzerhellung

Jaspers's very method, and the title of his second volume. Since Existenz never becomes an object, it cannot be known: it can only be illuminated — signalled toward, indirectly, so as to awaken it in the reader.

Not to be confused with a knowledge or a doctrine: nothing to learn, something to become.

Existenz →

Immanence

die Immanenz

The domain of what belongs to the world and can appear in experience. It is not to be despised: it is through the world, and only through it, that Transcendence becomes readable.

Not to be confused with the opposite of Transcendence. The two are not opposed: ciphers are immanent, and that is precisely why they speak.

Ciphers →

Individual

das Individuum

The particular human being in observable singularity. Not every individual is an accomplished Existenz — but every individual carries the possibility, without exception.

Not to be confused with Existenz. The individual is counted; Existenz is decided. That is why no one is ever a number.

Existenz →

Intellectual integrity

die Redlichkeit

Radical honesty in thought: distinguishing without cheating what I know, what I infer, what I believe and what I do not know. The virtue Jaspers demands before all others.

Not to be confused with modesty. It is not self-deprecation — it is refusing to disguise a conviction as proof, or an ignorance as certainty.

Inwardness

die Innerlichkeit

The dimension of experience no outside gaze reaches. It is not a closed world: it becomes true only in action and communication.

Not to be confused with secrecy. An inwardness that refuses to risk itself outside is no depth — it is a shell.

Communication →

K

Knowledge

das Wissen

Objectively verifiable and transmissible cognition. Indispensable — and incapable, on its own, of answering the questions of freedom, guilt, meaning and Transcendence.

Not to be confused with truth. Knowledge is one of the forms of truth, not its whole. Confusing the two is the definition of scientism.

Truth →

L

Leap

der Sprung

The passage no continuous reasoning produces: in the boundary situation, one can tip from the existence that merely lasts toward the Existenz that answers. Nothing triggers it automatically.

Not to be confused with an irrational flight. It is no renunciation of thought: it is a change in the way of existing, with open eyes.

Boundary situations →

Limit

die Grenze

The point beyond which a method, a knowledge or a mastery cannot advance by its own means. Recognising it is not giving up thought: it is beginning.

Not to be confused with a provisional obstacle. Some limits do not retreat with progress — they separate not the known from the unknown, but two orders.

Loving struggle

der liebende Kampf

The form true communication takes: we question each other without complacency (struggle), but we struggle for the other, not against them (loving). All weapons are laid down: if either wins, both have lost.

Not to be confused with a debate to be won, nor a fusion in which differences dissolve.

Communication →

M

Mass

die Masse

Not the people nor the many: a way of existing in which one becomes interchangeable, and opinion, propaganda or the apparatus think in our place.

Not to be confused with the working class. It is a mode of life, not a social category — and cultivated people fall into it just as readily.

The spiritual situation of the age →

Meaning

der Sinn

The orientation by which a reality becomes intelligible for existence. The meaning of history cannot be demonstrated as a law: it depends on what human beings make of their freedom.

Not to be confused with a goal. A meaning is not a destination fixed in advance — it is a direction one can will, without guarantee.

Dossier · Does history have a meaning? →

Mechanization of existence

die Daseinsmaschinerie

The turning of collective life into a system of functions, procedures and outputs. Human beings then risk existing for the apparatus instead of the apparatus serving them.

Not to be confused with the automation of tasks. It is not the machine that worries Jaspers: it is the human being become a cog without noticing.

The spiritual situation of the age →

Metaphysical guilt

metaphysische Schuld

The deepest: there is a solidarity among human beings as human beings, and I am affected by every injustice committed in my presence in the world.

Not to be confused with a punishable offence. No court judges it — it is a wound of being, and it transforms rather than condemns.

Guilt →

Metaphysics

die Metaphysik

The third movement of his Philosophy (1932), after world orientation and the illumination of Existenz. It thinks the relation of Existenz to Transcendence by reading ciphers.

Not to be confused with a science of the beyond. It knows nothing: it interprets. None of its propositions can be proven, and it knows this.

Dossier · World and Transcendence →

Method

die Methode

A regulated way of knowing a domain. Every method has a legitimacy — and a territory. The danger begins when one of them declares itself universal.

Not to be confused with a neutral tool. A method decides what it can see: choosing it already decides what will be found.

Dossier · Man and nature →

Moral guilt

moralische Schuld

My responsibility before my own conscience: my acts, my silences, my compromises, my small cowardices. “An order is an order” is never an absolution.

Not to be confused with criminal guilt. The court has no right to judge me there — and I have no right to wait for it to.

Guilt →

Myth

der Mythos

A story that says in images what the concept cannot reach. It becomes a cipher when read as such — and a lie when taken for a news report.

Not to be confused with a fable to be liquidated. Throwing myths away because they are not facts deprives us of the only language we have for the absolute.

Ciphers →

N

Necessity

die Notwendigkeit

What imposes itself as law, condition or constraint. Existential freedom does not abolish it: freedom appears only in relation to it.

Not to be confused with fate. Necessity does not say what I shall make of it — and that is exactly where freedom lodges.

Freedom →

Non-knowledge

das Nichtwissen

The lucid recognition of what cannot be known. Not a provisional ignorance that progress would fill: the point beyond which any assertion would become illegitimate.

Not to be confused with ignorance or scepticism. It is a knowledge about the borders of knowledge — and it demands more rigour, not less.

Norm

die Norm

The rule by which a conduct or a knowledge is judged. Objective norms are necessary — they never replace the existential decision.

Not to be confused with living morality. Following a norm may be right; hiding behind it so as not to decide is exactly the opposite.

Freedom →

O

Objectification

die Objektivierung

The act by which a reality becomes an object for a subject. All science depends on it, and rightly so. The trouble begins when we assume that what cannot be objectified does not exist.

Not to be confused with a fault. Objectifying is legitimate and necessary — unless we believe the world is made the way our grasp of it is made.

Dossier · The subject-object split →

Objective truth

objektive Wahrheit

Truth founded on evidence and methods accessible to every rational consciousness. Compelling in its domain — and mute on the questions that concern me personally.

Not to be confused with the only truth. It is sovereign on a bridge, a vaccine, a historical fact. It has nothing to say about whom to love.

Truth →

Objectivity

die Objektivität

Validity independent of personal preference. Jaspers fully defends it within its domain — and refuses that it exhaust truth.

Not to be confused with the whole of truth. Objectivity is a truth, the most compelling one; it is not the only one.

Dossier · Consciousness in general →

Openness

die Offenheit

Readiness for what may shake my certainties. It does not require having no convictions: it requires not enclosing truth within mine.

Not to be confused with indifference. To be open is not to accept everything — it is to refuse to close.

Communication →

Orientation in the world

Weltorientierung

The first volume of his Philosophy (1932), and the first step of the path: learning, observing, knowing the sciences, understanding history — and discovering, in doing so, that knowledge never closes on a whole.

Not to be confused with a stage to be left behind: it remains necessary all one's life. Jaspers never abandons knowledge; he marks its limit.

The path →

Origin

der Ursprung

That from which a reality arises and receives its impulse. In his book on history, the origin means not merely a dated beginning, but the dark ground out of which humanity becomes historical.

Not to be confused with a starting date. The origin is not behind us like a fact: it is that from which we are still searching.

Dossier · At the origins →

P

Personality

die Persönlichkeit

The relatively stable set of my psychological traits. It can be observed, tested, described — and that is precisely why it is not Existenz.

Not to be confused with Existenz. Personality describes what I am; Existenz is what I can become. A test can map the first, never the second.

Existenz →

Philosophical faith

der philosophische Glaube

Between proven knowledge and received dogma: a trust won by thinking, firm enough to carry a life, humble enough to include doubt. Open to believers of every tradition and to atheists alike.

Not to be confused with one more religion, nor knowledge in disguise, nor a vague “spiritual but not religious”. It has precise contents — and proves nothing, which guards it from fanaticism.

Philosophical faith →

Philosophizing

das Philosophieren

An activity, not a content: questioning, clarifying, transforming oneself. One can know the whole history of philosophy without philosophizing once — and the reverse is true too.

Not to be confused with learning philosophy. Hence this site's motto: to philosophize is to be on the way.

The path →

Political freedom

die politische Freiheit

The legal and institutional conditions that allow speech, participation, criticism of power. It provides the outer space in which personal responsibility becomes effective.

Not to be confused with existential freedom. One can be politically free and never have decided anything — and the reverse: Jaspers was free under Nazism.

Freedom →

Political guilt

politische Schuld

I answer for the deeds of the state whose citizen I am, even having committed nothing: I lived under that order, profited from it or failed to prevent it. Its answer is reparation.

Not to be confused with an inherited shame. It is not a stain: it is a responsibility to be assumed, with concrete consequences.

Guilt →

Possibility

die Möglichkeit

What may become real. The human being is not only what they already are: they are also what they may become — and it is to that Jaspers speaks.

Not to be confused with an eventuality. It is not a probable future: it is what, in me, is waiting for a decision.

Existenz →

Prehistory

die Vorgeschichte

The immense period before writing. Remains yield practices; they will never yield the inner experience of the first human beings.

Not to be confused with a primitive time. It is the vast ground out of which history arises late — almost yesterday.

Dossier · Before history →

Process

der Prozess

In psychopathology: a psychic transformation that cannot be understood as the meaningful development of the previous personality. It can be explained without being understood from within.

Not to be confused with a development. It is a rupture — and the word marks exactly where understanding stops and explanation must take over.

Dossier · Man and nature →

Psychology of Worldviews

Psychologie der Weltanschauungen

His 1919 book, which scandalised the professional philosophers: it already spoke of boundary situations and shells. It is the book by which he entered philosophy.

Not to be confused with a work of psychology. It is the bridge between the physician and the philosopher — and nobody had asked for it.

The man →

R

Rationalism

der Rationalismus

The absolutisation of rational, objective thought. Jaspers does not reject reason: he rejects its reduction to a system claiming to know the absolute.

Not to be confused with rationality. Being rational is a virtue; rationalism is what happens when it believes itself alone in the world.

Dossier · Reason and communication →

Reality

die Wirklichkeit

What is effectively present. It appears differently according to whether we meet it as empirical existence, object of knowledge, spirit, Existenz, world or cipher.

Not to be confused with what is measurable. The same mountain is a fold of rock for the geologist and a cipher for whoever looks at it on a certain evening.

Truth →

Reason

Vernunft

Not the faculty of calculating, but a will: the will to stop at no mode, to stay open to all, to refuse every closure. It produces no knowledge; it prevents each mode from taking itself for the whole.

Not to be confused with the understanding that demonstrates (that is consciousness in general). It is a permanent refusal to be satisfied — the exact opposite of a system.

Dossier · Reason and communication →

Religion

die Religion

The historical and communal form of the relation to Transcendence: rites, stories, institutions, revelations. Jaspers acknowledges its depth and refuses it one thing only: monopoly.

Not to be confused with an adversary. He never fought religions — only their possible claim to possess what cannot be possessed.

Philosophical faith →

Responsibility

die Verantwortung

The obligation to answer for one's acts, omissions and share in a collective situation. It presupposes freedom — even when freedom is narrow.

Not to be confused with guilt. To answer is not to punish oneself: it is to recognise that something depended on me.

Guilt →

Revealed faith

der Offenbarungsglaube

Faith founded on a historical revelation, sacred texts, a tradition. Jaspers disputes neither its depth nor its lived truth — only its right to impose itself as universal knowledge.

Not to be confused with an adversary of philosophical faith. Jaspers debated with theologians all his life; what he refuses is obedience, not faith.

Philosophical faith →

Revelation

die Offenbarung

The historical manifestation of a divine truth recognised by a community. For philosophical faith it cannot become compelling knowledge — but it can become a cipher.

Not to be confused with a proof. Jaspers does not deny revelation: he denies that it can be imposed on those who do not receive it.

Ciphers →

S

Science

die Wissenschaft

The methodical pursuit of objective knowledge. Jaspers defends it against superstition and against ideology. Authentic science knows the limits of its method.

Not to be confused with scientism. Science says where it stops; scientism says nothing exists beyond. Jaspers defends the first against the second.

Dossier · Consciousness in general →

Scientific superstition

Wissenschaftsaberglaube

The precise moment when a method, legitimate in its own order, proclaims itself the total explanation of man. Science says: here is what my method reaches, and where it stops. Superstition says: what my method does not reach does not exist.

Not to be confused with a critique of science — it is exactly its opposite. It is a metaphysical belief disguised as a result.

Shell · Gehäuse

das Gehäuse

The system of ideas and certainties in which one takes shelter. It becomes a prison the moment one refuses all questioning and takes one's worldview for the whole truth.

Not to be confused with a conviction: a conviction exposes itself, a shell protects itself. The sign: whatever refuses to be questioned is already a shell.

Situation

die Situation

The concrete configuration in which I stand: possibilities, limits, ties, a history. I can change some of them — I can never live outside every situation.

Not to be confused with external circumstances. A situation includes me within it: it does not exist without someone to inhabit it.

Solitude

die Einsamkeit

The irreducible separation between beings: no one can decide, suffer or die exactly in my place. Fertile when it opens onto communication; a prison when it refuses it.

Not to be confused with chosen isolation. Jaspers offers no praise of withdrawal — solitude has meaning only as the condition of a true encounter.

Spirit

Geist

The mode by which I participate in wholes that precede me: a language, a culture, a work, an institution. It does not calculate, it understands; it does not demonstrate, it interprets. Its question: does this make sense within a whole?

Not to be confused with the ghost, the religious spiritual, or intelligence. Its weakness: wanting harmony, it shuts its eyes to what tears.

Spiritual situation of the age

die geistige Situation der Zeit

The 1931 diagnosis: the technical and bureaucratic apparatus, in order to function, must make everything calculable — ourselves included. The danger is not first of all catastrophe: it is functioning so well that we forget to exist.

Not to be confused with a condemnation of technology — Jaspers, a physician, owed it his life. What he refuses is that a means should set the ends.

Struggle

der Kampf

An unavoidable dimension of existence — one of the four boundary situations. Jaspers distinguishes the struggle for life, violent and necessary, from the loving struggle of communication.

Not to be confused with violence alone. To live is to take someone's place — even unwillingly. That is what makes it unavoidable.

Subject-object split

Subjekt-Objekt-Spaltung

The structure of all consciousness: the moment I think something, there is the one who aims and what is aimed at. Never one without the other, and never closed — a pole always remains uncaught: the one from which I catch.

Not to be confused with the structure of reality: nothing proves that. It is our way of grasping — and the Encompassing is that within which this cut occurs.

Subjectivity

die Subjektivität

The personal, lived dimension of experience. Jaspers refuses both its suppression by objectivism and its absolutisation in a relativism where each would hold a private truth.

Not to be confused with caprice. Subjectivity is not the opposite of rigour: there is a rigour of lived experience, and it is called communication.

Superstition

der Aberglaube

A belief that imitates knowledge without respecting its methods. Jaspers criticises two: the anti-scientific, and the scientific, which expects from science answers it cannot give.

Not to be confused with faith. Superstition disguises itself as knowledge; faith knows it is not one. That is the whole difference.

Technology

die Technik

The means by which human beings transform nature and organise their lives. It supplies means — never the ends those means should serve.

Not to be confused with an evil in itself. Jaspers, a physician, owed it his life. What he refuses: that a means should become the measure of all things.

Totalitarianism

der Totalitarismus

The system that seeks to control the totality of life: thought, language, history, ties. It first destroys free communication, and reduces each person to a function of the apparatus.

Not to be confused with a mere dictatorship. It is not only coercion: it is the claim to occupy the inside of heads as well.

Tradition

die Tradition

The inheritance of texts, practices and values transmitted through history. It lives when freely appropriated; it dies when it demands repetition without question.

Not to be confused with the past. A tradition is not what was: it is what one takes up — or what one recites.

Transcendence

Transzendenz

What carries everything without being carried by anything. Never an object, always a call. It is not proven: it is sensed, through Existenz (where I experience receiving myself) and through ciphers.

Not to be confused with the supernatural, nor a supreme being somewhere (that would be an object), nor a spatial “beyond”. Every tradition writes it in its own way; none owns the original.

Transcending

das Transzendieren

The movement of thought that goes beyond an object or a perspective without claiming to reach a new definitive object. Transcending opens; it does not conquer.

Not to be confused with Transcendence. One is a verb — a movement of thought; the other is what that movement signals toward.

Truth

Wahrheit

Not a possession but a way, on several levels: the useful (Dasein), the proven (consciousness in general), the meaningful (spirit), and the lived (Existenz) — true only when tested in communication. “Truth is what binds us together.”

Not to be confused with relativism (each level keeps its criteria) or scientism. Whatever presents itself as total, closed truth is already a lie.

U

Unconditional action

unbedingtes Handeln

The act performed not from interest, calculation or obedience, but from a demand recognised as absolutely binding — defending someone unjustly accused when it costs dearly.

Not to be confused with heroism. It seeks no witness and tells no story about itself: often nobody notices it.

Unconditionality

die Unbedingtheit

The mark of a commitment that depends neither on advantage, nor success, nor circumstance. It appears when betraying oneself would mean losing the very meaning of one's existence.

Not to be confused with stubbornness. The unconditional is not what I refuse to discuss — it is what I cannot abandon without ceasing to be myself.

Understanding

das Verstehen

Grasping from within the meaning of an experience: how humiliation leads to anger. The method Jaspers the psychiatrist distinguished from explanation, and from which his whole philosophy issues.

Not to be confused with explanation (see that entry). One explains causes; one understands meaning. Both are rigorous — in two different orders.

Unity of humanity

die Einheit der Menschheit

The conviction that human beings belong to a common history and responsibility despite the plurality of cultures. Unity does not mean uniformity.

Not to be confused with globalisation. Being interdependent is not being united: technology brought us closer without making us speak to one another.

Universal

das Allgemeingültige

What every rational consciousness can recognise. Jaspers distinguishes the compelling universality of knowledge from existential truth, which never imposes itself in the same way.

Not to be confused with unanimity. A universal truth is not what everyone thinks: it is what any mind must admit if it follows the method.

Universal communication

universale Kommunikation

The ideal of open speech among all people, all cultures, all faiths. It does not require thinking alike — only that no one claim to hold the truth alone.

Not to be confused with a world consensus. The unity sought is not agreement: it is the conversation that never closes.

Universal history

die Weltgeschichte

The attempt to think human history as a common whole, without reducing the diversity of peoples to a single model. The Axial Age is its thinkable axis.

Not to be confused with the history of the West enlarged. Against Hegel, the axis belongs to all — and nothing culminates there.

University

die Universität

The institution devoted to the search for truth through science, education and communication. Jaspers, who refounded Heidelberg's in 1945, refused to let it become a vocational school or an apparatus.

Not to be confused with a place for transmitting knowledge. Its function is to form judgement, freedom and responsibility — not to deliver skills.

Will

der Wille

The capacity to pursue an end consciously. It may serve the needs of the living creature, personal arbitrariness, or the unconditional action of Existenz.

Not to be confused with freedom. Willing strongly is not being free — a fanatic wills enormously.

World philosophy

die Weltphilosophie

His final project: a philosophy arising from communication among humanity's great traditions. Neither fusion nor an imposed Western model — a unity sought across the plurality of origins.

Not to be confused with syncretism. The project remained open and unfinished: Jaspers wanted it so.

Worldview

die Weltanschauung

The set of representations and values through which a person interprets reality. The subject of his 1919 book — the one by which he entered philosophy.

Not to be confused with a philosophy. A worldview becomes a shell the moment it closes and claims to contain the whole truth.
