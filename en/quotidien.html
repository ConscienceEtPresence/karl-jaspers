When it happens to you

Jaspers in everyday life

Sixteen situations — and what a serious philosophy can actually do about them

A philosophy good only for understanding philosophy would not be worth much. Jaspers did not want to manufacture specialists: he wanted to awaken someone. Here, then, are sixteen situations almost everyone goes through — and what his thought actually changes in them. Each is built the same way: the lived experience, then what Jaspers will not say (because false consolations must be cleared away before anyone can speak), then what he offers.

One thing first, very clearly

This is not therapy, and Jaspers would be the first to say so — he was a psychiatrist before he was a philosopher, and he never confused the two. If something is heavy, lasting, if you no longer sleep, if you are thinking of ending it: it is not a philosopher you need, it is a doctor, and now. Philosophy does not heal. It illuminates — which is not the same thing, and replaces nothing.

Sixteen situations

Each has its own page. Click the one that concerns you.

  1. “I have lost someone”This is not a difficulty. It is a wall.
  2. “I am afraid of dying”It comes at night, without warning, and it is not a thought: it is a coldness.
  3. “I am ill”The body decides, and you follow. Plans are put away, people become awkward, time changes texture.
  4. “My boss humiliates me”In front of the others, often.
  5. “He is the one who decides”At work, or at home. He decides, you follow. It is not violent, it is often pleasant.
  6. “I feel empty”Everything is fine, though. Work, health, people around you.
  7. “I analyse myself endlessly, and nothing changes”You know everything. Your childhood, your mechanisms, why you do that, where it comes from.
  8. “I cannot decide”You made the lists. You asked people. You read, compared, weighed.
  9. “I did something unforgivable”Not a mistake. A wrong. Something you did, or let happen, that no explanation repairs.
  10. “I no longer believe in anything”Not God, not progress, not the big words. You are not bitter — it is calmer than that.
  11. “I can no longer talk to him”You still speak. The salt, the schedule, what someone said.
  12. “I say nothing”You could have said it. The sentence was right there.
  13. “I am in conflict with someone”It has been going on for months. You are right, he is wrong — or the reverse depending on the day.
  14. “We argue, and nobody listens”Two hours of debate. Solid arguments on both sides.
  15. “Nobody understands me”You are surrounded. You talk all day.
  16. “I have failed at everything”A life's project, a marriage, a business. You had given everything, and it did not hold.
